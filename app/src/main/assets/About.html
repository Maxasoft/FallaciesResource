Purpose of Fallacies Resource

I have been a software engineer most of my adult life and I have always been fascinated by logic and how little people know about it and how to apply it. Nobody likes to think of themselves as illogical, and yet, the truth is that most people fall prey to biases, cognitive dissonance, unsound logic, and bad critical thinking when formulating their beliefs concerning big areas of discourse. Although, there is tons of material on the psychology, sociology and the science of why people make bad decisions, this resource is to help as a reference for fallacies (errors in logic).

I wanted something that was easy to navigate, but, above all, I wanted it to be available offline to save time and bandwidth.

Also, I have included links the actual pages used to extract the material found in this app. Although, it could be considered plagiarism, it is actually out of the most sincere humbleness, respect and gratitude that I have done this. These websites helped me hone in my critical thinking skills and I honestly hope that it will do so for the readers since I believe that only through unobstructed communication unhindered by illogical ideas, can humans make this planet a place where we can all live in peace and harmony.

Enjoy!

Luhit Young
